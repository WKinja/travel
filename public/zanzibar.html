Zanzibar City

Famous for historic Stone Town, spice farms, and pristine Indian Ocean beaches.

Description

Zanzibar City, located on the island of Unguja, is a cultural and historical gem. Its capital, Stone Town, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, with winding alleys, carved doors, and vibrant local life. The city is a gateway to exotic spice farms and turquoise beaches.

History

Zanzibar was a powerful trading hub for centuries, ruled by Arabs, Persians, and Portuguese. It became the center of the East African slave trade and later, a British protectorate. Today, its rich multicultural past is preserved in its architecture and traditions.

Attractions

  • Stone Town (UNESCO Site)
  • Prison Island
  • Spice Tours
  • Forodhani Gardens

Food

Enjoy Swahili delicacies, grilled seafood, and tropical fruits. Recommended places include:

  • Emerson on Hurumzi – Rooftop dining with culture and views
  • Lukmaan Restaurant – Local buffet-style favorite
  • 6 Degrees South

Nightlife

  • Forodhani Night Market – Open-air evening food market
  • Tatu Bar – Local DJs and cocktails
  • Livingstone Beach Restaurant – Sunset views and chill evenings
