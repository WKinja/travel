Mombasa

Historic coastal city with vibrant culture and white sandy beaches.

Description

Mombasa is a coastal city in Kenya with a rich blend of cultures, scenic beaches, and centuries-old Swahili architecture. Its relaxed coastal vibe and warm climate make it a popular destination for beach lovers, history buffs, and adventurers alike.

History

As one of the oldest and busiest ports in East Africa, Mombasa has been influenced by Arab, Portuguese, British, and Indian traders. Fort Jesus, built in 1593 by the Portuguese, stands as a testament to this multicultural past.

Attractions

  • Fort Jesus Museum
  • Nyali Beach
  • Haller Park
  • Mombasa Marine Park

Food

Try local delicacies such as Swahili biryani, grilled seafood, and bhajias. Recommended places include:

  • Tamarind Mombasa – Upscale seafood by the water
  • Yul’s Restaurant – Casual beachside dining
  • Blue Room – Iconic café serving Swahili and Indian cuisine

Nightlife

  • Tamarind Dhow – Floating restaurant and music
  • The Lounge Nyali
  • Sheba Lounge – A relaxed spot with cocktails and music

Where to Stay

  • Serena Beach Resort
  • Sarova Whitesands
  • Mombasa Airbnbs
