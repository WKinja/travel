Lamu

A peaceful island town known for timeless beauty, dhow boats, and Swahili culture.

Description

Lamu is an enchanting island off Kenya’s northern coast with no cars—just donkeys and boats. Its ancient architecture, narrow alleyways, and calm beaches offer a charming, timeless vibe. It's a unique destination for travelers who enjoy cultural depth and slow-paced living.

History

Lamu is one of East Africa’s oldest towns, dating back to the 14th century. A major Swahili port, it was a center of Islamic learning and trade. Its Old Town is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its preserved Swahili architecture and cultural influence.

Attractions

Walk through Lamu Old Town, visit the Lamu Museum, take a dhow trip at sunset, and explore Shela Beach’s sand dunes and calm waters.

  • Lamu Old Town (UNESCO)
  • Lamu Museum
  • Sunset Dhow Cruises
  • Shela Beach & Village

Food

Lamu cuisine is full of Swahili flavors like biryani, pilau, and seafood with coconut. Popular dining spots include:

  • Seafront Café – Famous for seafood and island-style dishes
  • Peponi Hotel Restaurant – Great cocktails and views
  • Lamu House Restaurant – Upscale dining with authentic Swahili meals

Nightlife

Nightlife is relaxed and cultural. Dhow cruises, rooftop hangouts, and acoustic performances are the norm.

  • Peponi Bar – Social evenings with a sea breeze
  • Manda Island Bonfire Nights – Available via local guides

Where to Stay

  • Peponi Hotel – Iconic family-run boutique hotel in Shela
  • Lamu House – Swahili-style rooms and a pool
  • The Majlis – Luxury beachfront resort on Manda Island
  • Lamu Island Airbnbs – Unique homes and beach villas
