Malindi

A lively coastal town that blends African, Arab, and Italian influences with beautiful beaches and history.

Description

Located along Kenya’s northern coast, Malindi is a vibrant beach town popular with both locals and international tourists. Its scenic beaches, coral reefs, and cultural fusion make it an ideal destination for relaxing, diving, and exploring historic sites.

History

Malindi was an important port in the 15th century, visited by explorers like Vasco da Gama. The Vasco da Gama Pillar still stands as a symbol of this colonial encounter. The town has strong Swahili roots influenced by Arab traders and later, European settlers.

Attractions

From ocean adventures to ancient ruins and natural wonders, here are some top attractions:

  • Malindi Marine National Park – Snorkeling, diving, and dolphin watching
  • Vasco da Gama Pillar
  • Hell's Kitchen (Marafa Depression) – A surreal canyon landscape
  • Falconry of Kenya – Bird sanctuary and animal center

Food

Malindi offers rich Swahili dishes with Italian culinary influences. Be sure to try tamarind prawns, grilled fish, and Italian gelato. Recommended places include:

  • Osteria Beach – High-end Italian cuisine by the sea
  • Baby Marrow – Popular for seafood and live music
  • Bar Bar – Great for breakfast, lunch, and coffee

Nightlife

Malindi has an active nightlife scene with beach clubs, bars, and live events:

  • Club Savannah – Dance club with DJ and themed nights
  • Fermento Lounge – Lounge bar with cocktails and music
  • Osteria Lounge – Offers a relaxed after-dinner vibe

Where to Stay

  • Diamonds Dream of Africa – Luxurious beachfront resort
  • Sandies Tropical Village – All-inclusive beachfront stay
  • Driftwood Club – Charming colonial-style resort
  • Malindi Airbnbs – Private homes and beach rentals
